16 More questions

We have learned that مَا “what” and مَنْ “who” can be used to ask questions.

مَا هَذَا؟ هَذَا كِتَاب.
“What is this? This is a book.”

مَنْ هَذِهِ؟ هَذَا مَرْأَة.
“Who is this? This is a woman.”

Arabic also has another word for “what”: مَاذَا.

مَاذَا is generally used with verbs instead of مَا.

مَاذَا رَكِب؟ رَكِبَ الدَّرَّاجَة
“What he rode?” = “What did he ride?”
“He rode the bike.”

We can use مَاذَا with prepositions before it.

فِي مَاذَا وَضَعْتَ الكِتِاب؟ وَضَعْتُ الكِتَاب في الحَقِيبَة
“In what you put the book?” = “In what did you put the book?”
“I put the book in th ebag.”

بِمَاذَا لَعِبَ الوَلَد؟ لَعِبَ الوَلَد بِالكُرَة.
“With what the boy played?” = “With what did the boy play?”
“The boy played with the ball.”

16.1 مَعَ

We already learned the word بِ which means “with”.

Arabic has another word مَعَ which also means “with”.

بِ is generally used if you do something with an object, e.g., you play with a ball, you open a door with a key.

NOTE TO INSTRUCTOR

For now, we will not consider usages of the type:

ذَهَبَ بِالرَّجُل و جَاءَ بِه
“To take someone or bring someone to …”

مَعَ is used when you do something together with someone else. For example,

ذَهَبْتُ إِلَى المَدْرَسَة مَعَ زَيْد
“I went to the school with Zayd.”

لَعِبَتْ البِنْت مَعَ زَيْنَب
“The girl played with Zaynab.”

مَعَ can also be used in a question:

مَعَ مَنْ لَعِبْتَ؟
“With whom you played?” = “With whom did you play?”
