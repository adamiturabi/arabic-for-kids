4 Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe nouns. Here are some adjectives in English:

  • big
  • small
  • good
  • fast

We can describe some nouns using these adjectives:

  • “a big house”
  • “a small cat”
  • “the good boy”
  • “the fast car”

Here are the Arabic adjectives:

Arabic English
كَبِير big
صَغِير small
حَسَن good
سَرِيع fast

In English we put the adjective before the noun, but in Arabic we put the adjective after the noun:

بَيْت كَبِير
“a big house”

كَلْب صَغِير
“a small dog”

However, adjectives are little more complicated in Arabic than in English. In Arabic, if the noun ends with a ة, then a ة is added to the adjective as well. For example,

قِطَّة صَغِيرَة
“a small cat”

When adding a ة, the letter before the ة always gets a fatḥah.

Adding ة to the adjective is also done when the noun refers to a girl or a woman, even if the noun does not have a ة.

“A mother” in Arabic is أُمّ. If we want to say “a good mother” we will say

أُمّ حَسَنَة
NOT
أُمّ حَسَنَ

Another rule for adjectives is that if the noun has ال “the”, then the adjective will also have ال:

الوَلَد الحَسَن
“the good boy” (We still say in English “the good boy”, not “the good the boy”)

Now how about if the noun has both ال and ة? Well, we add both ال and ة to the adjective as well:

السَّيَّارَة السَّرِيعَة
“the fast car”
