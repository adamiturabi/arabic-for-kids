An Arabic course for non-native children

1 Nouns

Nouns are words for people, animals, places or things. Here is an example of a noun: “a boy”.

In Arabic, “a boy” is وَلَد.

Note that in وَلَد, the و and ل have a fatḥah but د does not have any mark on it. For now, we will pretend that there is a sukoon on د so we will pronounce the word as walad.

Here are some more Arabic nouns:

English Arabic
a boy وَلَد walad
a lion أَسَد asad
a house بَيْت bayt
a book كِتَاب kitaab

In English we can say “a boy” or “the boy”. Arabic does not have a word for “a” but it does have a word for “the”.

The word for “the” in Arabic is اَلْ al.

اَلْ is joined to the noun. So “the boy” is اَلْوَلَد “al-walad”.

If an Arabic noun does not have اَلْ joined to it then in English we will say the noun with “a”.

  • اَلْوَلَد “the boy”
  • وَلَد “a boy”

Here are the nouns we saw earlier, with اَلْ:

Arabic English
اَلْوَلَد al-walad the boy
اَلْأَسَد al-asad the lion
اَلْبَيْت al-bayt the house
اَلْكِتَاب al-kitaab the book

NOTE TO INSTRUCTOR

It seems better to me to leave out الحروف الشمسية والقمرية for now. It should be okay if the student pronounces الدراجة as al-darraajah instead of ad-darraajah. In-shaa-Allah he will become familiar with the correct pronunciation when he reads it fully vowellized الدَّرَّاجَة and with Quranic tajweed instruction.

1.1 Vocabulary

NOTE TO INSTRUCTOR

The student should maintain a personal dictionary in a notebook. Set aside a few pages for:

  1. Nouns
  2. Verbs
  3. Adjectives
  4. Particles

Whenever new vocabulary is given the student should copy the new word and its meaning to his dictionary under the appropriate section.

Our first vocabulary list includes many words and the instructor may wish to give them to the student a few at a time.

If the students have been given Arabic instruction in pre-school they may already be familiar with many of these words.

Here is a word list to memorize.

Arabic English
وَلَد walad a boy
أَسَد asad a lion
بَيْت bayt a house
كِتَاب kitaab a book
قِرْد qird a monkey
كَلْب kalb a dog
قِطَّة qiṭṭah a cat
سَيَّارَة sayyaarah a car
دَرَّاجَة darraajah a bike
مَسْجِد masjid a masjid
تُفَّاحَة tuffaaḥah an apple
مَوْزَة mawzah a banana
بُرْتُقَالَة burtuqaalah an orange
بَيْضَة bayḍah an egg
مَدْرَسَة madrasah a school
كُرَة kurah a ball
قَلَم qalam a pen
حَدِيقَة ḥadeeqah a garden

NOTE TO INSTRUCTOR

In drills, when writing the definite article ال deliberately leave it without tashkeel in order to get the student familiarized with reading it.

Also, let the student write Arabic without any tashkeel on the letters. We will later ask him to vowelize only disambiguating letters, e.g. كتبتُ vs كتبتَ.
